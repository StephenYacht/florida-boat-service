Common Questions

Marine Audio FAQ

Do I need an amplifier for JL Audio marine speakers?
Technically no, but yes if you want them to perform. JL Audio marine speakers are significantly underutilized when powered directly from a head unit's internal amp. Adding a JL Audio XDM amplifier unlocks the speakers' full dynamic range — cleaner sound, better bass, and the ability to actually be heard over engine noise and wind. We always recommend amplification for any serious build.
What's the difference between the M3 and M6 series?
The M3 is JL Audio's mid-tier marine speaker — excellent performance at a more accessible price point. The M6 is their flagship line with higher power handling, a larger tweeter, greater output capability, and the Transflective™ LED lighting system. Both are exceptional; the M6 is for those who want the absolute best or who prioritize the lighted speaker aesthetic. We'll recommend the right series based on your system goals and budget.
Can I control Fusion audio from my Garmin chartplotter?
Yes — this is one of the best features of the Fusion / Garmin ecosystem. Compatible Fusion head units integrate with Garmin MFDs via NMEA 2000 using Garmin's OneHelm platform, allowing you to control volume, source, and zone selection directly from your chartplotter screen. We configure this integration as part of every Fusion installation on Garmin-equipped vessels.
How do the light controllers work with lighted speakers?
Light controllers like the JL Audio MLC-RW communicate with compatible JL Audio lighted speakers over a dedicated control cable. Once installed and paired, you can select colors, set zones, adjust brightness, and trigger lighting effects from the controller. We install the controller, run the control wiring cleanly through the vessel, and program your preferred presets before handing the system back to you.
Do you supply the equipment or do I buy it myself?
We supply everything as authorized dealers for JL Audio and Fusion. You get genuine products, correct factory warranty coverage, and the right components selected by installers who actually know the hardware — not a salesperson at a big box store who's never been on a boat. One call handles everything.
How long does a full marine audio installation take?
A basic stereo and speaker upgrade is typically a one-day job. A full multi-zone system with amplifiers, subwoofer, tower speakers, and a lighted speaker controller can take 2–3 days. We'll give you a precise timeline during your consultation once we've assessed your vessel and confirmed the scope of the build.
