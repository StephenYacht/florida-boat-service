Transducer Placement & Mounting

Proper transducer placement is critical — a poorly mounted transducer degrades performance regardless of the unit above it. We assess your hull, select the correct location, and mount it for clean water flow and maximum signal quality.

Why Proper Installation Matters
Sonar Is Only As Good As Its Install

Forward-facing sonar is the most installation-sensitive electronics category on a boat. Turbulent water flow from improper transducer placement, interference from nearby through-hulls, or incorrect tilt angle can render even a $2,000 LiveScope system nearly useless.

Our Airmar Certified and NMEA-certified installers assess your hull type, engine configuration, and existing through-hull locations before recommending transducer placement. We mount, cable-route, and configure your system for the best possible real-world performance — then take it on a test run to verify.

The Backbone of a Modern Helm

NMEA 2000 Network Integration

A properly designed NMEA 2000 network is what separates a collection of individual devices from a fully integrated marine electronics system — and we're certified to build it right.

NMEA 2000 is the industry-standard communication protocol that allows all your marine electronics to share data over a single network backbone. When properly installed, your chartplotter knows your engine RPM, your autopilot sees wind data, and your MFD displays depth from any sonar on the network.

As NMEA Certified Installers, we design and build your network from scratch — proper backbone sizing, correct termination, T-connector placement, and load calculations to ensure every device communicates reliably without dropouts or interference.

Whether you're adding one device or building a complete helm from the ground up, we ensure your NMEA 2000 network is built to last and easy to expand in the future.

Common Questions

Marine Electronics FAQ

Can you supply the equipment or do I need to buy it myself?
We supply everything. As an authorized dealer for Garmin, Simrad, Raymarine, JL Audio, and Fusion, we sell and install. You get one point of contact, correct product selection for your vessel, and full warranty coverage — no coordinating between a retailer and an installer.
How long does a full electronics installation take?
A single unit installation (one chartplotter or VHF) is typically a half day or less. A full helm build with multiple integrated devices, NMEA network, and camera systems can take 2–5 days. We'll give you a precise timeline during your consultation once we understand the scope of your project.
Do you install electronics on any size vessel?
Yes — we work on center consoles, sportfishing boats, motoryachts, and sailboats. From a 20-foot bay boat to a 100+ foot motoryacht, we've done it. We assess each vessel individually and bring the right tools and team for the job.
Can you upgrade my existing electronics without replacing everything?
Absolutely. Many installs are partial upgrades — adding a forward-facing sonar, upgrading a chartplotter, or adding Starlink to an existing system. We assess your current setup, identify what's compatible, and integrate new hardware cleanly into what's already working.
Do you offer any warranty on your installation work?
Yes. We stand behind every installation we complete. If anything related to our workmanship causes an issue, we make it right. Equipment itself is covered under the manufacturer's factory warranty, which is fully protected by our certified installer status.
What brands do you carry and install?
We're authorized dealers for Garmin, Simrad, Raymarine, JL Audio, and Fusion. We also install FLIR thermal cameras, Starlink, and other leading marine electronics brands. If you have a specific brand or model in mind, give us a call — chances are we can supply and install it.
